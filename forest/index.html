Environment Clearances in India: 2014-2020


This series was produced in partnership with the Pulitzer Center



The map shows the location of 2,053 projects out of a total of 2,115 projects that the Ministry of Environment, Forest and Climate Change (MoEFCC) granted Environment Clearances to between July 2014 and March 2020.

The maps also show Protected Areas (PAs) i.e. areas that enjoy a higher degree of protection to preserve their biodiversity (flora, fauna, ecosystems, etc) and for which human occupation is legally limited. National parks, wildlife sanctuaries, conservation reserves are all examples of PAs.

India’s four biodiversity hotspots are also shown on the map for reference; these are internationally recognised as being among the Earth’s 36 most biologically rich regions.

What is an EC: An Environment Clearance (EC) is an approval required before an organisation can start any infrastructure, mining, processing or manufacturing undertaking that may be potentially detrimental to the environment. The clearance is granted either by the Centre or the state depending on the type of project it is. In addition to an EC, some projects may require wildlife and forest clearances.

How to use the map: Explore the location and types of projects granted EC since 2014, by using the filters alongside the map. Discover which projects have been approved in each state, year, industry and their distance from nearby PAs.

About the data


Location coordinates have been used to map locations for 1,378 of the 2,053 projects that received ECs. Location coordinates were unavailable, or noticeably inaccurate (outside India, for example) for 795 projects. These have been plotted as per the provided tehsil information. Certain tehsils will therefore appear to have a large number of overlapping projects.

The remaining 62 projects have not been plotted on the map. These are projects for which location coordinates and tehsil information is missing in the Parivesh data.

Sources: We collected primary data from the Parivesh portal maintained by the MoEFCC. The 672 PAs were mapped based on information available on the UN-supported Protected Planet website. The 4 biodiversity hotspots present in India were collected from the Critical Ecosystem Partnership Fund, hosted on ArcGIS Hub.

Note: All efforts have been made to collect and map the data accurately. The primary data from Parivesh is frequently riddled with errors. For instance, certain projects are mapped in a neighbouring state and not the state in which it is supposed to be. Each project has been manually copied from the website and there could be inaccuracies. Kindly let us know if you see a data point in the wrong place. Please include the Proposal Name as given in the pop-up box to help us identify the project.

Reporting


Reporting for this project was done between the second half of 2019 and early 2020. IndiaSpend travelled across the country for four months, reporting from Uttar Pradesh to Karnataka, as a part of a year-long investigation on how some of India’s infrastructure projects are imperilling the environment.

Our ground reports will be added to the map and as and when they are published at IndiaSpend.com
